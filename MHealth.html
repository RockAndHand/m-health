Mental Health Self-Assessment

Disclaimer: This tool is for informational purposes only and does not replace professional advice. If you are in distress or crisis, please contact local emergency services immediately.

1. Little interest or pleasure in doing things?




2. Feeling down, depressed, or hopeless?




3. Trouble falling or staying asleep, or sleeping too much?




4. Feeling tired or having little energy?




5. Poor appetite or overeating?




6. Feeling bad about yourself — or that you are a failure or have let yourself or your family down?




7. Trouble concentrating on things, such as reading the newspaper or watching television?




8. Moving or speaking so slowly that other people could have noticed, or being so fidgety or restless that you’ve been moving around a lot more than usual?




9. Thoughts that you would be better off dead, or of hurting yourself in some way?




10. How often have you felt anxious or nervous, even when there is no clear reason?




11. How frequently have you experienced excessive worrying that you find hard to control?




12. How often have you noticed difficulty managing daily responsibilities because of your feelings?




13. Have you experienced sudden episodes of intense fear or panic?




14. How often have you felt irritable or on edge, even without a clear reason?




15. How often have you felt detached or disconnected from yourself or your surroundings?
